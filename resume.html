Build a resume that is easy to scan and easy to trust.

Your resume should quickly show employers what you have done, what skills you used, and what results you created. Use this page to move from a rough draft to a polished, tailored document.

Resume checklist

  1. Start from a reliable format: use the UMSI guide so structure and spacing are already working for you.
  2. Choose relevant experiences: include jobs, internships, projects, coursework, and leadership that support your goal.
  3. Write results-focused bullets: lead with action verbs and show scope, tools, or outcomes when possible.
  4. Tailor to the role: match important keywords and emphasize the most relevant skills first.
  5. Review before sending: check formatting, grammar, consistency, and readability on mobile and desktop.

Common resume mistakes to avoid

Too general

Replace vague bullets with specific actions, tools, and accomplishments that help employers picture your work.

Too crowded

Use strong headings, clear spacing, and concise bullets so a recruiter can scan the page in seconds.

Not tailored

A single resume rarely fits every opportunity. Adjust your summary of experience to fit the position.

Unclear impact

If you improved something, explain how. Even a small metric or outcome makes your experience stronger.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be? For most students and new graduates, one page is the strongest default.
  • Can I include coursework and class projects? Yes. UMSI coursework can be highly relevant when described clearly.
  • Should I make a different resume for every application? Yes. Tailoring is one of the fastest ways to improve results.

Networking is easier when you know what to say next.

Strong networking is not about collecting contacts. It is about building real, respectful relationships with people who can help you learn, grow, and discover opportunities.

Open the Networking Guide Find a Career Event

What good networking looks like

  • Start with curiosity: ask thoughtful questions about work, teams, and career paths.
  • Be specific: mention how you found the person or why you want to connect.
  • Keep it manageable: one well-written message is better than five rushed ones.
  • Follow up professionally: send a thank-you note and keep the relationship warm over time.

Simple plan for outreach

  1. Identify a person whose role, employer, or path connects to your goals.
  2. Write a short message with a clear introduction and one specific reason for reaching out.
  3. Ask for a brief conversation or one focused question instead of a broad request.
  4. Prepare two or three questions before the conversation begins.
  5. Send a thank-you note and record what you learned.

Helpful conversation prompts

At events

“Hi, I’m a UMSI student interested in your work. What skills do you use most in your role?”

In outreach emails

“I found your profile through UMSI alumni and would value 15 minutes to learn about your path into this field.”

For follow-up

“Thank you for your time. Your advice about project storytelling was especially helpful, and I plan to use it this week.”

For informational interviews

Ask about tasks, interests, relationships, and advice so the conversation stays focused and useful.

Networking FAQ

  • What if I feel awkward? That is normal. Use a short script, then focus on curiosity instead of performance.
  • How soon should I follow up? Within 24 to 48 hours is a strong default after an event or conversation.
  • Do I need LinkedIn? It helps, but respectful email outreach and in-person conversations still matter.
